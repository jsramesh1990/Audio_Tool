Building a board equivalent to a Toradex Verdin i.MX 8M Plus module **completely from scratch** is a large hardware project. Companies like Toradex spend years designing, validating, and certifying these modules. However, if your goal is to design your own SoM or carrier board around the NXP i.MX 8M Plus, here is the typical process.

## 1. Understand the Architecture

A Verdin i.MX8M Plus module consists of:

```text
+------------------------------------------------+
|               i.MX 8M Plus SoC                 |
|                                                |
| 4x Cortex A53                                  |
| Cortex M7                                      |
| NPU (2.3 TOPS)                                 |
| GPU + Video Codec                              |
+------------------------------------------------+

       |---- LPDDR4 RAM
       |---- eMMC Flash
       |---- PMIC
       |---- Ethernet PHY
       |---- WiFi/BT Module
       |---- Audio Codec
       |---- Oscillators
       |---- USB PHY
       |---- PCIe
       |---- HDMI / MIPI DSI
       |---- Camera CSI
```

The SoM is then connected to a carrier board exposing connectors and peripherals.

# 3. Main Components

A minimal i.MX8M Plus SoM contains:

### Processor

* NXP i.MX8M Plus

Package:

* FCBGA
* 0.65 mm pitch
* ~0.8 mm ball diameter

This is difficult to hand solder.

---

### LPDDR4 RAM

Typical:

* 2 GB
* 4 GB
* 8 GB

Examples:

* Micron LPDDR4
* Samsung LPDDR4

Requirements:

* Length matched traces
* Controlled impedance
* DDR timing tuning

DDR routing is among the hardest parts.

* Kingston

# 4. Power Tree Design

The power architecture is critical.

Example:

```text
12V input

   |
Buck Converter
   |

+-----------------------+
| PCA9450 PMIC          |
+-----------------------+

0.8V --> Cortex A53
0.9V --> GPU
1.1V --> DDR
1.8V --> IO
3.3V --> peripherals
```

Requirements:

* Proper startup sequencing
* Brownout protection
* Thermal management

An incorrect power sequence can permanently damage the SoC.

# 6. DDR Layout

This is the most difficult section.

Requirements:

### Length Matching

Example:

```text
DQ0
DQ1
DQ2
DQ3

All lengths
must match
within
a few mils.
```

Clock:

```text
CLK+
CLK-

Must be:

matched
differential
controlled impedance
```

Failure results in:

* Boot failures
* Random crashes
* Memory corruption

# 7. High-Speed Interfaces

You must route:

### USB 3.0

Requirements:

* 90 Ω differential
* Equal lengths
* Minimal vias

---

### PCIe

Requirements:

* Differential pair
* 85 Ω
* Tight skew control

---

### Gigabit Ethernet

Requirements:

* 100 Ω differential
* Isolation transformer
* PHY placement rules

---

### HDMI

Requirements:

* TMDS routing
* ESD protection
* Controlled impedance

# 10. Manufacturing Challenges

Building this board yourself requires:

### PCB fabrication

* 10–12 layer HDI board
* Microvias
* Blind vias
* Controlled impedance

---

### Assembly

You need:

* BGA placement machine
* Reflow oven
* X-ray inspection

Because:

* i.MX8M Plus is a fine-pitch BGA.
* LPDDR4 is also BGA.

Hand soldering is generally impractical.

# 12. Recommended Learning Path

If your goal is eventually to design a Verdin-class SoM:

1. Start with an evaluation board based on the i.MX8M Plus.
2. Design a simple carrier board:

   * USB
   * Ethernet
   * Audio codec
   * LCD
3. Learn:

   * DDR routing
   * Power sequencing
   * High-speed PCB design
   * Linux BSP development
4. Build a custom carrier board first.
5. Finally attempt a custom SoM.

For most startups and product teams, the common approach is:

* **Step 1:** Use a commercial SoM like Verdin.
* **Step 2:** Design your own carrier board.
* **Step 3:** Move to a custom SoM only when production volume is very high (typically tens of thousands of units).

This approach dramatically reduces risk, development time, and certification effort.
